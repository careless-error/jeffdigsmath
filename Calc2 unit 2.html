Chapter 5 – Sequences and Series

5.1 – Sequences

Sequence

A sequence is a list of numbers in a specific order:

\[ \{a_n\} = a_1, a_2, a_3, \dots \]

Arithmetic Sequence

\[ a_n = a_1 + (n - 1)d \]

d: common difference

\[ d = a_{n+1} - a_n \]

Diverges unless \( d = 0 \)

Geometric Sequence

\[ a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n - 1} \]

r: common ratio

\[ r = \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \]

Converges if \( |r| < 1 \); diverges if \( |r| \ge 1 \)

Recursive Formulas

\[ a_{n+1} = a_n + d \quad \text{(arithmetic)} \] \[ a_{n+1} = r \cdot a_n \quad \text{(geometric)} \]

Convergence

\[ \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = L \Rightarrow \text{sequence converges} \]

Bounded Sequences

  • Bounded above: \( a_n \le M \)
  • Bounded below: \( a_n \ge m \)
  • Bounded: both above and below

Monotonic Sequences

  • Increasing: \( a_{n+1} \ge a_n \)
  • Strictly increasing: \( a_{n+1} > a_n \)
  • Decreasing: \( a_{n+1} \le a_n \)
  • Strictly decreasing: \( a_{n+1} < a_n \)
  • Monotonic: increasing or decreasing

Monotonic Sequence Theorem

If a sequence is bounded and monotonic, it converges.

Important Limits

\[ \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n^p} = 0 \quad (p > 0) \] \[ \lim_{n \to \infty} r^n = 0 \quad (|r| < 1) \] \[ \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{n^p}{e^n} = 0 \quad (p > 0) \]

5.2 – Infinite Series

Series

\[ \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n \]

Defined as the limit of partial sums:

\[ S_n = a_1 + a_2 + \dots + a_n, \quad \lim_{n \to \infty} S_n = L \]

Terminology

  • \( a_n \): general term
  • \( S_n \): partial sum
  • Convergent: \( \lim S_n = L \) (finite)
  • Divergent: no finite limit

5.3 – Divergence and Integral Tests

Test for Divergence

\[ \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n \ne 0 \Rightarrow \sum a_n \text{ diverges} \]

If the limit equals zero, the test is inconclusive.

Geometric Series

\[ \sum_{n=0}^\infty ar^n = \frac{a}{1 - r} \quad \text{if } |r| < 1 \]

Diverges if \( |r| \ge 1 \)

Harmonic Series

\[ \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n} \]

Diverges

Integral Test

If \( f(x) \) is positive, continuous, and decreasing on \( [N, \infty) \), and \( a_n = f(n) \), then:

\[ \sum a_n \text{ and } \int_N^\infty f(x)\,dx \text{ both converge or both diverge} \]

5.4 – Comparison Tests

Direct Comparison Test

If \( 0 \le a_n \le b_n \):

  • If \( \sum b_n \) converges, \( \sum a_n \) converges
  • If \( \sum b_n \) diverges and \( a_n \ge b_n \), then \( \sum a_n \) diverges

Limit Comparison Test

\[ \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_n}{b_n} = L, \quad 0 < L < \infty \Rightarrow \sum a_n \text{ and } \sum b_n \text{ both converge or both diverge} \]

Common Comparison Targets

  • \( p \)-series: \( \sum \frac{1}{n^p} \), converges if \( p > 1 \), diverges if \( p \le 1 \)
  • Geometric series: \( \sum ar^n \), converges if \( |r| < 1 \)

5.5 – Alternating Series

Alternating Series

\[ \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^n a_n \quad \text{or} \quad \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} a_n \]

Where \( a_n > 0 \) for all \( n \ge N \)

Alternating Series Test

The series converges if, for all \( n \ge N \):

  • \( a_n > 0 \)
  • \( a_n \) is decreasing
  • \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 0 \)

Absolute Convergence

\[ \sum a_n \text{ is absolutely convergent if } \sum |a_n| \text{ converges} \]

Conditional Convergence

\[ \sum a_n \text{ is conditionally convergent if it converges but } \sum |a_n| \text{ diverges} \]

5.6 – Ratio and Root Tests

Ratio Test

\[ L = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \right| \]
  • If \( L < 1 \): converges absolutely
  • If \( L > 1 \) or \( L = \infty \): diverges
  • If \( L = 1 \): inconclusive

Root Test

\[ L = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sqrt[n]{|a_n|} \]
  • If \( L < 1 \): converges absolutely
  • If \( L > 1 \) or \( L = \infty \): diverges
  • If \( L = 1 \): inconclusive
