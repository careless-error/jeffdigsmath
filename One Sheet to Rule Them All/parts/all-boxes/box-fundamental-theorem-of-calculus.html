Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
If \( f \) is continuous on \([a, b] \) and \( F' = f \), then:

\[ \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a) \]
The definite integral gives the total change in \( F \) over \([a, b]\).

Net Change Theorem: If \( f \) is a rate, the integral gives total accumulation or change.

Applications: displacement, total cost, accumulated quantity
